2004, June ??

  • ACS 1.1 is released, and contains:
    • Three new volumes in the Kali series:
      • 4. User Interface
      • 5. Plummer's Model
      • 6. Speeding Up Ruby
    • We extended, in Kali, volume 7. Self-Describing Data Files
    • Hermite integrators, in Kali, volume 2, chapters 9, 10, and in volume 3, chapter 13 [code present, description as yet incomplete]

2004, June 5

  • ACS 1.0.1 is released, and contains:
    • A sixth-order integrator, described in Kali, volume 2, chapter 5, and in volume 3, chapter 11
    • postscript and pdf files, that were left out inadvertently from ACS 1.0

2004, June 2

  • ACS 1.0 is released, and contains:
    • several new volumes, including roughly 500 pages of new text
    • a dialogue between two researchers, rather than between the three students as was the case in Moving Stars Around
    • a switch from C++ to Ruby
    • a clear plan for extending the current material to construct a new simulation code for dense stellar systems, the Kali code, in the course of the next several years.

2003, March 19

  • For an N-Body School in Strassbourg, France,
    • we listed the manuscript Moving Stars Around as required reading
    • we helped the students to adapt the various ACS source codes to their own projects

2003, October 7

  • We made the manuscript Moving Stars Around available on the ACS web site. It contains:
    • a dialogue between three students, who start setting up N-body codes from scratch, using the C++ computer language
    • roughly 270 pages of text, in postcript, pdf, and html format
    • all C++ codes and scripts mentioned in the text

2003, September 18

  • We acquired the domain name "ArtCompsSci.org", and started to build the ACS web site.
