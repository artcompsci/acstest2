The Art of Computational Science

The Kali Code for Dense Stellar Systems


© 2003-2004 Piet Hut and Jun Makino Back to ACS home page.
The Kali project is directed toward the construction of a laboratory for dense stellar systems, such as star clusters, star forming regions, and galactic nuclei containing one or more massive black holes. The presentation is centered around dialogues between two astrophysicists, Alice and Bob, who are in the process of constructing the Kali code from scratch.
  • 0. A Vision: Dealing with Dense Stellar Systems    (PS  /  PDF)
    • in which Alice and Bob discuss the growing need for simulations of dense stellar systems.
    • They wonder how to give students hands-on access to this exciting area of astrophysics.
    • Alice is ready to build some toy models; Bob remains skeptical whether that's really useful.
  • 1a. The 2-Body Problem: Forward Euler, in Ruby    (PS  /  PDF)
    • in which Alice and Bob explore the scripting language Ruby, as an alternative to Fortran or C++.
    • they use Ruby to define a vector class and build a very simple 2-body integrator.
    • Bob is still skeptical, but he is also getting more curious.
  • 1b. The 2-Body Problem: Higher-Order Integrators    (PS  /  PDF)
    • in which Alice and Bob play with higher-order integrators.
    • Bob gets carried away, implementing one integrator after another, up to eighth order.
    • They wind up with a Hermite scheme, multi-step methods, and symplectic integrators.
  • 2. The N-Body Problem: From Leapfrog to Runge-Kutta    (PS  /  PDF)
    • in which Alice and Bob construct various N-body codes, up to fourth order, with fixed time steps.
    • Alice reminds Bob of their plan to build toy models, rather than a plethora of cool integrators.
    • They get a few basic N-body integrators to work, and strongly feel the need for graphics.
  • 3. An N-Body Lab: Softening, Cold Collapse, Graphics    (PS  /  PDF)     [incomplete]
    • in which Alice and Bob have fun doing astrophysics experiments with their new tools.
    • They introduce some simple graphics, but realize the need for far more infrastructure.
    • After some debate, they decide to provide a robust foundation for their virtual lab.
  • 4. User Interface: Command Line Arguments    (PS  /  PDF)
    • in which Alice and Bob design a flexible interface, including command line options.
    • In the process, they add a detailed help facility and general documentation option.
    • This is the first step, in laying their lab foundation.
  • 5. Initial Conditions: Plummer's Model    (PS  /  PDF)
    • in which Alice and Bob create a standard star cluster model for starting N-body simulations.
    • In the process, they learn a lot about potential-density pairs, phase space, dimensional analysis.
    • This is the second step, in laying their lab foundation.
  • 6. A Matter of Speed: C Modules for Ruby    (PS  /  PDF)     [incomplete]
    • in which Alice and Bob tackle the slow speed of Ruby, as a scripting language.
    • They achieve good speedup, in several different ways that are easy to implement.
    • This is the third step, in laying their lab foundation.
  • 7. ACS Data Format: Self-Describing Data Files    (PS  /  PDF)     [incomplete]
    • in which Alice and Bob tackle the problem of unstructured piles of data.
    • They design a structured ACS data format, including a story mechanism for self-describing data.
    • This is the fourth step, in laying their lab foundation.
  • 7c. Diagnostics: Simulating a Lab Assistant    (PS  /  PDF)
    • in which Alice and Bob write a program to characterize an N-body snapshot.
    • They have fun playing with concepts from artificial intelligence.
    • This is the fifth step, in laying their lab foundation.
  • 8. N-body Codes: A Plethora of Algorithms    (PS  /  PDF)     [incomplete]
    • in which Alice and Bob implement the collection of algorithms that they derived in volume 1b.
    • With good speed, a flexible interface and structured data, it is now fun to run experiments.
    • This is the sixth step, in laying their lab foundation.
  • 9. Graphics: Graphs and Movies     [in preparation]
    • in which Alice and Bob start building a collection of graphics tools.
    • Besides producing graphs, they use Ruby and OpenGL to make interactive N-body movies.
    • This is the seventh step, in laying their lab foundation.
  • 10. Open Knowledge: A New Form of Literate Programming     [in preparation]
    • in which Alice and Bob settle on a standard way to document their lab building process.
    • They hit upon the `open knowledge' concept as an extension of the notion of `open source.'
    • This is the eighth and final step, in laying their lab foundation.
  • 11. Gravothermodynamics: The Low-N Limit, with Softening     [in preparation]
    • in which Alice and Bob start their first research project, using their newly completed virtual lab.
    • They play with systems of softened self-gravitating particles, enclosed in a reflecting sphere.
    • They are surprised how much physics can be learned from such simple systems.
  • 12. Dynamic Control: Variable Time Steps     [in preparation]
    • in which Alice and Bob start experimenting with point particles.
    • They witness the formation of binaries, and how these slow down the simulations to a crawl.
    • They conclude that the binary bottleneck can only be overcome with individual time steps.
  • 13. Dense Stellar Systems: Individual Time Steps     [in preparation]
    • in which Alice and Bob give each particle its own integration time step size.
    • They experiment with block time step schemes, and different ways to speed things up.
    • To their delight, they can now easily handle collisional systems with hundreds of particles.
  • 14. Core Collapse: The Low-N Limit     [in preparation]
    • in which Alice and Bob explore the thermodynamics of core collapse with point particles.
    • They analyze N-dependence and scaling with space and time, and compare that with theory.
    • They realize that binary formation leads to unacceptable energy errors.
  • 15. Binary Formation: Detection, Evolution, and Feedback     [in preparation]
    • in which Alice and Bob confront the problem of binary formation head-on.
    • They implement an analytic Kepler treatment, as a quick fix to speed up hard binary evolution.
    • They manage to go beyond core collapse, but realize the need for much better binary treatment.
